01 — The Paradigm Shift

Robotics is Having Its GPT Moment

Vision-Language-Action models are separating the intelligence layer from hardware. The value is migrating from atoms to bits.

Before VLAs (Pre-2023)

  • Robots programmed task-by-task
  • Separate perception, planning, control modules
  • Months of engineering per new task
  • Zero generalization to new objects
  • $100K+ per deployment

After VLAs (2024+)

  • Single model: camera → language → motor commands
  • End-to-end learned from demonstrations
  • ~1 hour of robot data per new task (GEN-1)
  • Compositional generalization to novel tasks (π0.7)
  • ~99% success rate vs 64% prior

The Big Bet: Investment in world models surged from €1.3B (2024) to €6.5B (2025) — a 5x increase in one year. VLAs now power ~40% of new robot deployments.

04 — The India Opportunity

A 40x Density Gap Is a Greenfield

India's robot density of 30 vs South Korea's 1,220 isn't a weakness — it's the single biggest structural argument for robotics in India.

Robot Density (per 10K manufacturing workers)

India's Gap = India's Opportunity

4.4x below global average • 5.5x below China • 40x below South Korea

Structural Advantages

  • PLI scheme driving electronics/semiconductor factory buildout
  • Domestic robots at $12K–$18K vs $40K+ imports
  • DST grants for indigenous hardware development
  • Defense budget ₹7.85L Cr (FY2026, +9.5%)
  • ARTPARK @ IISc — startup incubation & R&D bridge
  • Young, tech-savvy workforce ready for upskilling

Structural Challenges

  • ~60% of robot cost = imported components (China dependent)
  • After-sales service is brutal across Tier 2/3 cities
  • MSMEs price-shop aggressively — ROI must be obvious
  • Skills gap in mechatronics and system integration
  • Long enterprise sales cycles (6–18 months)
